Understanding Gender-Based Violence

What is Gender-Based Violence?

Gender-based violence (GBV) refers to harmful acts directed at an individual based on their gender. It is rooted in gender inequality, the abuse of power, and harmful norms. GBV can include sexual, physical, mental, and economic harm inflicted in public or in private. Examples include domestic violence, sexual harassment, rape, human trafficking, and harmful practices such as female genital mutilation and child marriage.

Impact of Gender-Based Violence

The impact of GBV can be devastating and long-lasting. Victims may suffer from physical injuries, mental health issues such as depression and anxiety, and social stigma. GBV can also have severe economic consequences, including loss of income and financial dependence on the abuser. It is important to recognize that GBV affects not only the victim but also families, communities, and society as a whole.

How to Stay Safe

1. Know Your Rights: Understanding your legal rights can empower you to take action against abuse. Familiarize yourself with local laws and resources available for GBV victims.

2. Create a Safety Plan: If you are in an abusive situation, having a safety plan can help you escape safely. This includes having a trusted friend or relative to contact, knowing where to go, and keeping emergency numbers handy.

3. Seek Support: Reach out to support groups, hotlines, and local organizations that can provide assistance and guidance. Do not hesitate to ask for help.
